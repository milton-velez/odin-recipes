Tacos de Carne Asada

Description

These carne asada tacos made with tender strips of flank steak, served on soft corn tortillas are as good as you get at a taqueria. So delicious topped with onion relish and homemade tomato salsa!

Ingredients

Marinade

  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika

Salsa

  • 4 dried New Mexico chile pods
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 white onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 jalapeño peppers, chopped
  • 1 pinch salt and black pepper

Ingredients

  1. Place the flank steak in a large glass baking dish.
  2. Prepare the marinade: Whisk together soy sauce, oil, vinegar, 4 cloves of garlic, juice of two limes, salt, black pepper, white pepper, garlic powder, chili powder, oregano, cumin, and paprika in a bowl. Pour over the steak, coating both sides. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 to 8 hours.
  3. Prepare the relish: Stir onion, cilantro, and lime juice together in a small bowl and set aside.
  4. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C). Toast chile pods in a skillet over medium-high heat for a few minutes, then soak them in a bowl of water for 30 minutes.
  5. Place tomatoes, 1 onion, 4 cloves of garlic, and jalapeños on a baking sheet. Roast in the oven for about 20 minutes until toasted.
  6. Transfer the roasted vegetables and soaked chiles into a blender or food processor. Add salt and pepper, then puree until smooth.
  7. Remove the steak from the marinade (discarding the excess) and cut into cubes or strips. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat and cook the steak until the liquid evaporates and it is cooked through.
  8. Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet over low heat for approximately one minute per side.
  9. Assemble: Place the steak on the tortillas, top with relish and salsa, sprinkle with cotija cheese, and garnish with lime wedges.
